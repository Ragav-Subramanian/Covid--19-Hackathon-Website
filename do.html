DO'S & DONT'S DURING COVID19 OUTBREAK

DO’s

1. Do wash your hands before you wear your mask.

2. Do cover your cough or sneeze. The best way to cough or sneeze is into your elbow and not your hands.

3. Do work from home. Do leave your home only for essential reasons, such as buying food or medications.

4. Do cancel elective dental, medical, recreational and non-essential appointments as these can interfere with social distancing.

5. Do move away from others who cough or sneeze.

6. Do wipe down all surfaces you come into contact with. Any regular bleach-containing household products is likely to be effective.

7. Do call ahead to the doctor/emergency room before you head there if you think you may have symptoms concerning for COVID-19. You should also wear a facemask to protect others.

8. Do wear a face mask and gloves if you are caring for a family someone who has tested positive for COVID-19.

9. Do be mindful of what you surfaces you are touching and how frequently you are touching your face.

10. Do protect your immune system by getting your vaccinations, eating a balanced diet, avoiding too much alcohol.

11. Do participate in “social distancing” to blunt the spread of the virus.

12. Do remember that virus may live on surfaces like cash or credit cards. Wash your hands thoroughly after handling cash or credit cards before touching your face.

13. Do stay home and take Tylenol, cough syrup and plenty of rest if you think you may have COVID-19.

14. You should also isolate yourself from your other family members, and use a designated bathroom in the house that no one else is using.

15. Do look for red flags, like shortness of breath, fever >102F and worsening symptoms.

16. Do seek medical care if you develop any of these symptoms at any time.

17. Do self-quarantine yourself for 14 days if you come into contact with someone who tests positive for coronavirus.

DONT’S

1. Don’t just tie a scarf around your face as that can be loose fitting and need more adjustment. Use a cloth covering that is more tight-fitting.

3. Don’t travel within the United States or abroad.

4. Don’t touch your eyes, nose, mouth or face without washing your hands.

6. Don’t interact with your pets if you think you may have symptoms concerning for COVID-19.

7. Don’t treat “social distancing” like a “vacation”. This is not the time to run errands or go to the mountains.

8. Don’t plan “play dates” for your kids if they are not in school.

10. Don’t shake hands or give hugs as greetings.

11. Don’t visit your older relatives or community members, as they are the highest risk group.

12. Don’t go to public places like gyms, theaters, restaurants.

13. Don’t call for testing if you don’t have symptoms as testing is not recommended by CDC and the White House Task Force if you don’t have symptoms.
